Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that originated in Rome. It's made with spaghetti, eggs, pancetta or bacon, Pecorino Romano cheese, and black pepper. The dish is simple yet flavorful, with the creamy sauce created by whisking together eggs and cheese. The salty pancetta adds depth of flavor while the black pepper gives it a spicy kick. Carbonara has become a popular dish around the world due to its delicious taste and easy preparation. It's a perfect meal for any occasion and can be enjoyed by everyone who loves pasta!
